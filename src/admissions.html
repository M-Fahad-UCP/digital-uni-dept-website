Admissions

Degree Programs

  • Bachelor of Science in Software Engineering
  • Master of Science in Software Engineering
  • Ph.D. in Software Engineering

Admission Criteria

Undergraduate applicants follow university admission rules. Graduate applicants require a relevant undergraduate degree, statement of purpose, letters of recommendation, and transcripts.

Minimum GPA and standardized test requirements vary by program and year — consult the graduate admissions page for details.

Application Steps & Deadlines

  1. Prepare transcripts and recommendation letters.
  2. Submit online application and required documents.
  3. Deadlines: Undergraduate — July 31; Graduate — March 15 (Fall admission).

Application Checklist

  • Completed application form
  • Official transcripts
  • Statement of Purpose (500–1000 words)
  • Two or three letters of recommendation
  • CV / resume

International applicants: include proof of English proficiency and check visa timelines.

Funding & Scholarships

Graduate assistantships and merit-based scholarships are available. Financial support decisions are made during the admissions review.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the admission deadlines?

Undergraduate: July 31. Graduate (Fall): March 15. Check program pages for special deadlines.

Is financial aid available?

Yes — assistantships and scholarships. International students may apply for limited funding.

Can I email faculty directly about research?

Yes — prospective graduate students should contact faculty whose research matches their interests.
